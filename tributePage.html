Anna May Wong

The first Chinese America Hollywood movie star

Photo of Anna May Wong from a German postcard by Ross Verlag

A time line of Anna May Wong's life:

  • 1905 - Born in Los Angeles, California, U.S.A
  • 1921 - Dropped out of high school to pursue a full-time acting career
  • 1922 - Played her first lead role in The Toll of the Sea
  • 1924 - Gained attention of the public due to a supporting role in The Thief of Bagdad
  • 1928 - She left Hollywood for Europe due to being typecast and getting passed over for Asian roles by non-Asian actresses
  • 1929 - She made her last silent film called Piccadilly
  • 1930 - Paramount offered her a contract, and she made a return to Hollywood
  • 1936 - She began a year long tour in China
  • 1938 - The Chinese Benevolent Association of California honored her for her support of Chinese refugees
  • 1942 - She starred in two anti-Japanese proganda films, Bombs over Burma and Lady from Chungking
  • 1949 - Wong had a small role in a film called Impact after a six year hiatus
  • 1951 - Wong starred in a detective series called The Gallery of Madame Liu-Tsong
  • 1956 - She hosted a U.S documentary on China
  • 1960 - She received a star on the inauguration of the Hollywood walk of fame
  • 1961 - She died on February 3rd at the age of 56

"This is such a short life that nothing can matter very much either one way or another. I have learned not to struggle but to flow along with the tide. If I am to be rich and famous, that will be fine. If not, what do riches and fame count in the long run?"

--Anna May Wong
